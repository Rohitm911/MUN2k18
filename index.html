About us

Being Symbolic to contemporary thought, The Cathedral institution was established in 1866 with a purpose to educate orphans and empower female education. Today, The Cathedral High School and Pre-university College stands out as one of the best co-educational institution in Bengaluru, especially for its academic excellence and holistic approach of education. With an exemplary Campus, this institution has been home to various fests and co-curricular activities like NIE Quizzes, Model United Nations, Catheon and sportstop. Through these co-curricular activities we not only fulfil our goal of holistic development of students but also reach out to students of various institutions and foster their potential for the good of the society as a whole. .

Committees

The Economic and Financial Committee (ECOFIN)

The Globalisation of labour, sounds peculiar, almost a typo even. Yet therein lies an agenda that shakes the world economy to its core. Globalisation has indeed changed the world, an interconnected web of constant interdependence is what we envision. Yet it seems that this journey has many a hurdle. Wages for labour that are essentially the same see stark contrasts between nations of developed and developing statuses.

In this committee, we seek to device a way out of this pit. Thereby narrowing the wage gap, reducing geographic isolations and strengthening the world economy. From policy’s that establish safety nets to low income wages of developing nations, to the eradication on tax loopholes that favour corporations, we hope to see innovative outcomes to this very issue.

Agenda: Globalisation of Labour.


International Monetary Fund (IMF)

Stay away from it. It’s a mirage, basically. - Warren Buffet.


The global economy is interconnected in a way that is unprecedented in human history. With the emergence of Bitcoin and all the over 1,000 Crypto-coins, financial transactions are being decentralized on a massive scale. These decentralized transactions have no recourse whatsoever to the US Dollar or any other currency for that matter. This Changes the dynamics of international trade, foreign relations, diplomacy, and the impact of economic sanctions.

Bitcoin, Ethereum, Ripples, Litecoin and many other cryptocurrencies continue to experience massive growth in price, market capitalization, and mainstream adoption. The cryptocurrencies are providing features and functions that are changing (maybe even improving) the way we do things. Until now, it’s been easy to scoff at the cryptocurrency boom, or ignore if it none were interested. But as the rally has spread from bitcoin to other coins, from those other coins to companies touting links to cryptocurrencies, and potentially soon those companies bidding for resources in the real economy, we’re going to see cryptocurrencies impact the real economy. It’s time to take notice, and to be afraid.

It no longer remains a question of whether cryptocurrencies are disrupting the global economy, but by how much and what the future holds. Time will tell how far this revolution will go.

Agenda 1: Regulation and challenges of Cryptocurrencies.

Agenda 2: Role of the IMF in the regulation of the Euro-zone crisis.


United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

The greatest threat to our planet is the belief that someone else will save it.


While the entire world is guzzling fossil fuels and trying to meet their energy requirements, Africa has continued to not only face energy crisis but also the issue of lack of the necessary infrastructure itself. While this is a definite setback for the development of various industries, it is also an opportunity for the development of robust green technology throughout the continent. Most countries in Africa remain underdeveloped or developing and as a result, demands international assistance and have led to numerous conferences, dialogues and debate but the outcome of all of them remain either too ambitious or too modest. There still remains a lack of a satisfactory resolution, but one thing remains certain and that is the development of the African Continent. The question is, will it happen through the well-set and environment destructive path of fossil fuel or by carving a new pioneering path of sustainable development.

According to a study by the Ocean Cleanup project, 8 million tonnes of plastics enter the ocean every year. They also found out that the Great Pacific Garbage Patch (GPGP) is now bigger than France, Germany and Spain combined. Oceans are the lifeblood of our planet and affect all of our lives in numerous ways. Oceans are also the biggest carbon sinks and are the only reason global warming hasn't been as catastrophic as it could have been. We have put the very element in danger that distinguishes us from the entire galaxy and upon which the survival of not just mankind but all living creatures depends. If we fail to act, we would have failed as a civilization.

You have the power to make a difference, there is the possibility, can you ensure the survival of mankind itself? Presenting to you the 4th committee at the CHS MUN 2018, United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP).

Note: UNEP is a double delegation committee.

Agenda 1: Implementation of green technology in African Nations

Agenda 2: Addressing the rising issue of marine litter


International Emergency Summit 2018 : Ethiopia (IES)

It’s not always the war for freedom which is glorious but always the sustainment of it.


Colonialism and imperialism has plagued this great continent. Now as freedom, sovereignty and liberty dawn upon the nations of Africa, there is still a predicament. Catastrophical events prevail here not because of the lack of independence but because of the greed for entitlement. As struggle for freedom embark in these nations, opportunistic rebellions with large potentials dwell into the arenas of conflicts to achieve their subjects of interest. While seizure takes place and rebellions encapsulate power, there is a holocaust. Devastations in these zones of conflict not only have a drastic impact on the peace and security of this continent, but also on the peace and security of the world. Crashing economies, failing governments, widespread poverty and daunted state of terror

If this isn’t an emergency.

Then what is?

Agenda 1: Reviewing the actions of The United Nations Mission In South Sudan (UNMISS)

Agenda 2: Insurgency in Africa


Ad Hoc Security Council

The United Nations Security Council eludes to various rules of Bushido. Such as never strike first and never strike unless strictly necessary. This springs from the fact that while the Security Council is the most powerful organ of the UN operating at the highest level of international diplomacy it is imperative that the council keep its responsibility for peace at the forefront. With that CHSMUN presents to you the Ad Hoc Security Council.
